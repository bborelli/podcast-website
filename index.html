Critics at Large

About the Podcast podcasts

"Critics at Large" is a weekly culture podcast from The New Yorker that dives into today's cultural obsessions, classic works viewed through a fresh lens, and the latest trends shaping books, television, film, and more. Every Thursday, staff writers Vinson Cunningham, Naomi Fry, and Alexandra Schwartz lead lively, thought-provoking discussions that blend deep analysis with behind-the-scenes insights from The New Yorker's world-renowned reporting. Together, they help listeners make sense of our cultural moment—and how we got here.

I've always felt we could learn more about ourselves by looking into art and culture. Maybe that's why I've always been so passionate about these subjects, even in my early days of journalism. From the moment I discovered The New Yorker, I dreamed of becoming a cultural critic, exploring the nuances of art, literature, and human behavior. Over time, my career and studies evolved, but my passion for these themes only deepened. This podcast feels like someone took my thoughts and crafted them into a brilliant series. Each episode resonates with my love for critical analysis and thoughtful conversation, guiding me through cultural trends, profound discussions, and fresh perspectives. Critics at Large is truly a blend of everything I value and aspire to in the world of culture and storytelling."

Meet the Hosts mic

Vinson Cunningham arrow_drop_down

Vinson Cunningham joined The New Yorker as a staff writer in 2016. Since 2018, he has served as a critic for the magazine, writing about theatre, television, and more. He was a finalist for the Pulitzer Prize for criticism in 2024, and was awarded the George Jean Nathan Award for Dramatic Criticism for 2021-2022. And, in 2020, he was a finalist for a National Magazine Award for his Profile of the comedian Tracy Morgan. He teaches at the Yale School of Art and Columbia University's School of the Arts, and is a co-host of Critics at Large, The New Yorker's weekly podcast about culture and the arts. His début novel, “Great Expectations,” came out in 2024.

Naomi Fry arrow_drop_down

Naomi Fry, a staff writer at The New Yorker since 2018, is a regular contributor to the weekly column Critic's Notebook. Her writing on popular culture, books, and art has appeared in the Times Book Review, the Times Magazine, Artforum, and n+1, among other publications. She attended Tel Aviv University, holds a master's degree in English from Johns Hopkins University, and has taught at New York University and the Rhode Island School of Design. She grew up in Israel, and now lives in Brooklyn.

Alexandra Schwartz arrow_drop_down

Alexandra Schwartz joined The New Yorker as a member of the editorial staff in 2013 and has been a staff writer since 2016. She has written extensively about literature as both a critic and a reporter, profiling writers such as Jennifer Egan, Miriam Toews, Vivian Gornick, and Ayad Akhtar. In 2014, she was awarded the National Book Critics Circle's Nona Balakian Citation for Excellence in Reviewing. Schwartz, who has written frequently on France, covered the November, 2015, terror attacks in Paris. She has also written pieces about New York City's newest neighborhood, the pitfalls of self-help, and the world's largest coöperative grocery store.
